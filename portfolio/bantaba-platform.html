Project

Improving the UX of Bantaba's Platform

    Company

    Bantaba

    Role

    UX & UI Designer

    Date

    April-June 2022

BACKGROUND

Bantaba is a platform that connects startups in Africa with Africans who have moved out of the continent and enables them to invest, mentor or consult the startups.

The company was born in 2020 from the idea of leveraging resources in the African diaspora to empower Africa’s tech ecosystem. They compare themselves with Tinder but with a different type of love – a love for Africa.

THE PROBLEM

In early 2022, Bantaba released a new version of their platform with a reworked UI and with new functions. However, according to surveys and interviews, users still weren’t very satisfied and asked for an improved UX.

So, how do we improve the UX of Bantaba’s platform?

THE PROCESS

  1. Impact Map
  2. Persona creation
  3. WCAG & UX Review
  4. User Tests
  5. User Journey Map
  6. Brainstorms
  7. Wireframes
  8. Prototype
IMPACT MAP

I made a basic Impact Map where I mapped out the primary stakeholders (African diaspora and tech startups in Africa) and their basic needs. I continued to update the map during the course of the projects, adding in more needs, and secondary stakeholders.

I went for an Impact Map as it'd be a constant reminder for me what the stakeholders' needs are.

PERSONAS

I created three personas that would help me envision the startups’ needs.

At first I wasn’t sure if I wanted to create startups or users as personas, and I ended up doing both for the most realistic and effective persona; for each persona, I also made a startup. This way, I could put myself in the individual’s shoes, as well as the rest of the startup.

One of the personas I made. I based each individual and startup on Bantaba's users to make sure they'd be relevant.

The reason I made personas is simply because I am not in our target group, and I thought personas could help with that. For instance, if I'm unsure about a feature, I could ask myself, "What's the value for Cheryl if we do [...]?". It ended up making things easier for me in the project as it helped make things less abstract.

WCAG & UX REVIEW

I hadn’t been aware Bantaba was an ancient word meaning ‘meeting underneath a tree’ and refers to a traditional tree-sanctuary where the elders of an African village gather to make important decisions. Including this would be a concrete way of telling the user that we’re here to help them meet - under the tree of Bantaba - to support each other and grow.

For this, I ended up putting together an illustration.


Wireframing

Once we were satisfied and knew what we wanted to include, I made a wireframe while the content strategist wrote all of the content. I used the websites I had looked at before as inspiration when I puzzled together boxes that’d represent the future content.




Difficulties

More often than not, startups need to develop quickly. Since Bantaba needed the landing page to be released asap, our front end dev started coding it immediately as designed. Hence, I couldn’t go back and change anything, and I had to be really quick with my designs.

I hope time won’t be such an issue again to the point where I need to design as the developer is simultaneously coding my design. Iterating becomes very difficult that way.
